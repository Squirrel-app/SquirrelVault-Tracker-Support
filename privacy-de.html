Summary

  • We collect the minimum account info from your sign-in provider (Google, Apple, or Facebook): email, display name, avatar (if any), and a technical account ID.
  • Your inventory data (items) and notes, as well as categories/groups, may be stored in the cloud (Firebase Firestore) to support account-based features.
  • If you use AI Scan, we process recognized text (OCR) to generate autofill suggestions. The app does not upload your photos as part of AI scan.
  • We currently do not serve ads and do not sell personal data to third-party marketers.

Data We Collect

Account information

When you sign in with Google, Apple, or Facebook, we receive your email, display name, avatar URL (if available), and a technical account ID. This is used only to authenticate you and secure access to the app.

App data (cloud)

When you are signed in, we store app data in the cloud (Firebase Firestore), such as items you create, item notes, categories/groups, and related metadata (e.g., creation and update timestamps). This helps operate account-based features.

App data (local)

Some app data is also stored locally on your device for performance and offline use. If you sign out, uninstall the app, or use in-app clear options, local data may be removed depending on the action.

AI scan data

If you use the AI scan feature, we process recognized text (OCR) from the product photo to generate autofill suggestions. The app does not upload your photos as part of AI scan.

Device and technical information

We may store basic technical metadata linked to your account (e.g., device family/model and iOS version) to help with security, debugging, and reliable operation of the app.

Crash and error reports

We use Firebase Crashlytics (provided by Google) to collect crash reports and non-fatal error data. This helps us identify and fix issues in the App. The following data may be collected automatically when the App encounters an error or crash:

  • Crash stack traces and error messages (technical details about what went wrong).
  • Device model, operating system version, and available memory.
  • Application state at the time of the crash (e.g., current screen).
  • Your account identifier (to help us investigate issues reported by specific users).
  • Breadcrumb logs — a limited sequence of app events leading up to the crash.

This data is used solely to improve the App's stability and is not used for advertising, tracking, or profiling. Crash data is processed by Google under its Firebase terms. You can learn more at Firebase Privacy.

How We Use Data

  • Authenticate your account and keep sessions secure.
  • Store and display your inventory and notes associated with your account.
  • Send notifications you opt in to (e.g., expiry reminders).
  • Process AI scan requests to generate item autofill suggestions (when you choose to use this feature).
  • Improve the app’s reliability and performance.

We currently do not sell your personal data. We do not access your email or contacts.

Sharing

We do not currently share personal data with third-party advertisers or data brokers. Service providers process data on our behalf as needed to operate the App. We may share anonymized or aggregated data that cannot reasonably be used to identify you.

  • Google Firebase — authentication (Firebase Auth), cloud database storage (Firestore), backend processing (Cloud Functions), and crash/error reporting (Crashlytics) required to operate account and cloud features and improve app stability.
  • OpenAI — processes OCR text to generate item autofill suggestions when you use the AI scan feature.
  • Open Food Facts — community food product database queried when you scan barcodes to retrieve product information (name, brand, category, etc.). Only the barcode is sent; no personal data is shared.
  • Apple — Sign in with Apple; anonymous crash reports (if you opted in on your device).
  • Meta (Facebook) — authentication (Facebook Login).

We may disclose information if required by law or legal process, or to protect our rights, safety, or property. We may use anonymized, aggregated data for analytics and product improvement that does not identify you.

Third-Party Data Sources

Open Food Facts

When you scan a product barcode, the App may query the Open Food Facts (OFF) database to retrieve product information such as name, brand, category, quantity, and nutritional data. Open Food Facts is a free, open, collaborative food products database licensed under the Open Database License (ODbL).

When querying Open Food Facts, we send only the product barcode and a technical User-Agent header identifying the App. No personal data (such as your name, email, or account information) is transmitted to Open Food Facts.

Product data retrieved from Open Food Facts is stored locally on your device and, if you are signed in, in your cloud account as part of your inventory items. This data is used solely to populate item details for your convenience.
